Colin McRae's Life, Career and Untimely Death

  • 1986: Colin entered the Scottish Rally Championship in a Talbot Sunbeam.
  • 1988: After winning the Scottish Rally championship in 1987, Colin entered his first WRC Rally race in a Vauxhall Nova.
  • 1991: Colin went professional with Team Prodrive which put him behind the wheel of his first Subaru. Earned his first British Rally Championship.
  • 1992: Earned his second British Rally Championship and graduated to the Subaru Factory Team in the World Rally Championship, aka: WRC.
  • 1993: Won first WRC Rally in New Zealand.
  • 1995: Won Drivers WRC Title against team mate Carlos Sainz in a thrilling end of season showdown at Rally of Great Britian.
  • 1998: Final year with Prodrive and the Subaru Factory Team he has become so well known for. Placed 3rd in WRC Drivers Stanings.
  • 1999: Joined M-Sport a Ford Factory Team earning 6 million pounds over 2 years. Netting him as the highest paid rally driver at the time.
  • 2000: Relliability concerns plague Colin's second year with M-Sport's Ford Focus rally cars. Does not place in the top 3.
  • 2001: Placed 2nd in overall drivers standings after crashing out in the final race of the season. Lost the title by 2 points overall.
  • 2002: At the end of the season Colin and M-sport/Ford split ways.
  • 2003: Colin joins Citroen for a 1 year stint, next to now historic rally drivers Carlos Sainz and Sebastian Loeb.
  • 2004: Without a drive for WRC, Colin looked elsewhere and drove the 24 Hours of Le Mans, and Dakar Rally.
  • 2005: Returned to Dakar Rally only to crash out.
  • 2007:
    • Colin still looking for a seat in the 2008 WRC season, stating that if it doesn't happen next year, then I won't (return) because you can only be out of something at that level for so long.
    • September of 2007, Colin passes away in helicopter accident at age 39.
